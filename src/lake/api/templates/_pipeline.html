{# The four ingest stages. Shared by the dashboard and /about — one copy, so the story cannot drift between the two pages. data-reveal drives the stagger and the blue rule that draws under each stage as it enters. #}
01

Collect

A scraper fetches bytes to local staging on its own schedule — daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly. Anything that fails a structural check is quarantined, never promoted.

02

Land

Bytes are checksummed and moved into the raw archive with a single atomic rename, read-only. Raw data is immutable: it is never edited, only superseded by a later run.

03

Catalog

Every run, file, and checksum is recorded in Postgres. That catalog is what answers whether a source went quiet or a scraper broke — and it drives the freshness alerts.

04

Serve

DuckDB validates and rebuilds each partition into typed Parquet, then publishes a read-only replica. That replica is what this site queries.
